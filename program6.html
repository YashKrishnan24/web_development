Founded by the visionary **Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj** in 1674, the Maratha Empire challenged the Mughal hegemony and re-established self-rule (*Swarajya*). Known for their speed, intelligence networks, and the "Ganimi Kava" (guerrilla tactics), they transformed from a regional force into the primary power of 18th-century India.

Territorial Extent

At its zenith (1758), the empire's saffron flag fluttered from Peshawar (modern-day Pakistan) in the north to Thanjavur in the south, and from Attock in the west to Cuttack (Odisha) in the east. It covered approximately 2.5 million square kilometers.

Shivaji Maharaj

1674 â€“ 1680

The Founder. A brilliant military strategist and administrator who carved out an independent kingdom from the declining Adil Shahi sultanate of Bijapur. He was crowned Chhatrapati in a grand ceremony at Raigad.

He established a competent civil rule with the help of a disciplined military and well-structured administrative organizations. He pioneered guerrilla warfare methods, which leveraged strategic factors like geography, speed, and surprise.

The Defender. The eldest son of Shivaji, Sambhaji faced the full might of the Mughal Empire led by Aurangzeb. In his short reign of 9 years, he fought over 120 battles and is reputed to have never lost a single one.

He was eventually captured and executed by Aurangzeb, an event that galvanized the Maratha resistance rather than crushing it.
