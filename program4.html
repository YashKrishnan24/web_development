The Economic Shockwave

The global economy experienced its deepest recession since World War II. In 2020 alone, global GDP contracted by 3.4%. Supply chain disruptions led to skyrocketing inflation in subsequent years.

The Silent Pandemic

Beyond the virus, a mental health crisis emerged. The World Health Organization (WHO) reported a 25% global increase in prevalence of anxiety and depression during the first year of the pandemic.

Social isolation, fear of infection, and grief created a "parallel pandemic" that affected younger demographics most severely.

Women and young people were disproportionately affected, with youth depression rates doubling in some high-income countries.

Long COVID: Chronic Impact

Approximately 10-20% of infections result in "Long COVID," a condition with over 200 reported symptoms affecting multiple organ systems.

This has created a new category of chronic illness, significantly impacting the global workforce and increasing the burden on healthcare systems.
